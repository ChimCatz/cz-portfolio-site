I Built My Portfolio Without Knowing How to Code (And It Still Worked)

April 23, 2026

Everyone says you need a portfolio.

Recruiters look for it. Professionals recommend it. It is almost expected now.

But from my perspective, that advice assumes something.

That you already know how to build one.

I did not.

No front-end experience. No design background. No real knowledge of HTML, CSS, or JavaScript.

So the real problem was not building a website.

It was figuring out where to even begin.

At first, I explored everything.

Wix. WordPress. Full coding from scratch.

Each one had its own advantages. Each one had its own learning curve.

And the more I explored, the more it felt overwhelming.

Too many options.

So nothing moved.

Then I shifted the question.

Not what tool should I use?

But why do I want a website in the first place?

Not for clout. Not to impress people.

I wanted to build something.

And I wanted to enjoy the process of learning it.

So I decided to work with what I already had.

VS Code. Codex and Claude. Git and GitHub Pages. Free assets from the internet.

No domain cost. No advanced stack. No polished design system.

Just execution.

From my perspective, this is where everything changed.

I did not need to know how to write everything manually.

I just needed to know what I wanted to build.

Instead of coding line by line, I described the layout. I explained the behavior. I adjusted based on what I saw.

Then the tools translated that into working code.

This was not new to me.

It followed the same pattern as the tools I built before:

20 to 30 minutes to under 20 seconds workflows.

60 minutes to under 15 seconds automation.

Hours of manual work reduced by more than 90%.

The website became just another system.

Not perfect. But working.

There were still problems.

Layouts broke. Mobile views looked off. Some designs did not feel right.

But instead of stopping, I kept going.

Fix. Test. Adjust.

Then repeat.

Along the way, I realized something.

I was not just building a website.

I was building skills.

Git helped me track changes and gave me a safety net. GitHub gave me free hosting and version control. AI tools helped me translate ideas into actual structure.

Things I did not plan to learn.

But ended up learning anyway.

The biggest lesson was not technical.

It was mental.

Fear shows up when you are working on something new.

You do not know what to do. You do not know how it will turn out. You do not know how others will see it.

From your perspective, that feels like a roadblock.

But it is not.

It is part of the process.

Looking back, the process was simple.

Start. Break. Fix. Repeat.

No perfect plan. Just progress.

I do not know everything.

But I used what I do know.

And built something out of it.

For anyone thinking of starting something, a website, a project, a tool, even a small idea, you do not need to have everything figured out.

You just need to begin.

Like my boss says:

"Skills can be learned. But mindset and character need cultivation."

From my perspective, this was not just about building a website.

It was about taking that first step into something unfamiliar and realizing that you can figure things out along the way.
