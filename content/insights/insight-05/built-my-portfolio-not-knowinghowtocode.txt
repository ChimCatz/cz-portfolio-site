---

**Title:**
I Built My Portfolio Without Knowing How to Code (And It Still Worked)

**Date:** April 23, 2026

---

Everyone says you need a portfolio.

Recruiters look for it. Professionals recommend it.
It’s almost expected now.

But from my perspective, that advice assumes something.

That you already know how to build one.

I didn’t.

No front-end experience.
No design background.
No real knowledge of HTML, CSS, or JavaScript.

So the real problem wasn’t building a website.

It was figuring out where to even begin.

---

At first, I explored everything.

Wix.
WordPress.
Full coding from scratch.

Each one had its own advantages. Each one had its own learning curve.

And the more I explored, the more it felt overwhelming.

Too many options.

So nothing moved.

---

Then I shifted the question.

Not *what tool should I use?*
But *why do I want a website in the first place?*

Not for clout.
Not to impress people.

I wanted to build something.

And I wanted to enjoy the process of learning it.

---

So I decided to work with what I already had.

VSCode.
Codex and Claude.
Git and GitHub Pages.
Free assets from the internet.

No domain cost.
No advanced stack.
No polished design system.

Just execution.

---

From my perspective, this is where everything changed.

I didn’t need to know how to write everything manually.

I just needed to know what I wanted to build.

Instead of coding line by line, I described the layout.
I explained the behavior.
I adjusted based on what I saw.

Then the tools translated that into working code.

---

This wasn’t new to me.

It followed the same pattern as the tools I built before:

* 20–30 minutes → under 20 seconds workflows 
* 60 minutes → under 15 seconds automation
* Hours of manual work → reduced by more than 90%

The website became just another system.

Not perfect. But working.

---

There were still problems.

Layouts broke.
Mobile views looked off.
Some designs didn’t feel right.

But instead of stopping, I kept going.

Fix. Test. Adjust.

Then repeat.

---

Along the way, I realized something.

I wasn’t just building a website.

I was building skills.

Git helped me track changes and gave me a safety net.
GitHub gave me free hosting and version control.
AI tools helped me translate ideas into actual structure.

Things I didn’t plan to learn.

But ended up learning anyway.

---

The biggest lesson wasn’t technical.

It was mental.

Fear shows up when you’re working on something new.

You don’t know what to do.
You don’t know how it will turn out.
You don’t know how others will see it.

From your perspective, that feels like a roadblock.

But it isn’t.

It’s part of the process.

---

Looking back, the process was simple.

Start → Break → Fix → Repeat

No perfect plan.

Just progress.

---

I don’t know everything.

But I used what I do know.

And built something out of it.

---

For anyone thinking of starting something:

A website.
A project.
A tool.
Even a small idea.

You don’t need to have everything figured out.

You just need to begin.

---

Like my boss says:

**“Skills can be learned. But mindset and character need cultivation.”**

From my perspective, this wasn’t just about building a website.

It was about taking that first step into something unfamiliar.

And realizing that you can figure things out along the way.
